Implementing IFRS 16

What does it mean for Leases?

With the introduction of IFRS 16, Companies will be required to classify a large number of leases as “Finance lease”, and not “Operating lease” as previously done under IAS 17.

First time adoption will require a thorough review of all contracts and then evaluating the same with various conditionalities of IFRS 16.

Complex calculations are necessary where leases contain fluctuating periodic rentals, ongoing changes in extension termination options and different currencies.

Effective Jan 01,2019, the way leases are accounted for by lessees in terms of recognition, measurement, presentation and disclosure has changed

Basic spreadsheet models created for individual leases present a greater risk of input error, and require significantly more manual time and effort to aggregate balances, report consistently and perform audits or verifications.

Functionalities

First time adoption module: Y2019 is the transition year in which all leases will be accounted for under IFRS 16 after having previously been treated under IAS 17/GAAP. The transition process is made easier by this tool as the user can input information relating to leases and it will calculate all of them in accordance with IFRS 16.

New leases module: This module will enable user to enter the information relating to any new leases, and will compute Interest expense, right-of-use asset, lease liability and depreciation in a matter of seconds.

Journal entries: This module will generate journal entries for the periods in one click.

Reports: By using different filters, the reporting module will identify leases by class of asset, location, period and expiry date.
